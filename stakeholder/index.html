Interview Guide
1.What's working well? What have you relied on IT for successfully?
2.How would you describe your current experience with IT support overall?
3.What are the biggest pain points or frustrations with IT?
4.What do you need from IT that you're not currently getting?
5.What systems do you use most day-to-day?
6.If you could change one thing about how IT operates, what would it be?
7.Any upcoming projects or changes IT should know about?
8.Do you know how to submit a ticket or reach IT when you need help?
9.What does a high-performing IT department look like to you?
You're speaking with
Opening: "Hi, this is Monique — I'm a business analyst working on an IT improvement initiative across NACS. I'm having quick conversations with leaders at each facility to understand how things are working from your side. Do you have about 10 minutes?"

Let them lead. If something interesting comes up, follow it — don't rush back to the list. "What does that look like day-to-day?" goes further than any scripted question.
