# Editorial Guidelines — NACSI / NAHCI / NACS Site
> Purpose: Defines the voice, tone standards, and red-flag copy patterns for the site editorial review agent.
> These guidelines reflect what this organization's site should sound like — and what it should never sound like.

---

## Voice Principles

The copy on this site should feel like it was written by a person who genuinely cares, not by a marketing department trying to fill space. The audience — families making deeply personal decisions about the care of a loved one — is smart, emotionally heightened, and has a low tolerance for vague reassurances.

**The copy should be:**
- Specific over general (say what you actually do, not that you "provide exceptional care")
- Warm without being saccharine
- Direct — get to the point, then back it up
- Human — written to a real person, not at an abstract "family"
- Grounded — every quality claim should be backed by a concrete detail

**The copy should never be:**
- Corporate: buzzwords, mission-statement filler, or language that could apply to any care org
- Pushy: urgency tactics, fear appeals, or stacked CTAs
- Vague: "we are committed to quality" tells nobody anything
- Self-congratulatory: leading with awards and accolades before earning the reader's trust
- Clinical where warmth is needed, or casual where professionalism matters

---

## Red Flag Phrases

The agent should flag any of the following on sight. These are not automatically wrong — some can be saved with specific supporting detail — but they all require scrutiny.

### Superlatives & Unverifiable Claims
Flag and require proof or removal:
- "the best," "best-in-class," "world-class," "nation's finest," "premier"
- "industry-leading," "cutting-edge," "state-of-the-art," "innovative"
- "unmatched," "unparalleled," "unsurpassed," "second to none"
- "exceptional," "superior," "outstanding" — unless immediately followed by a specific example

### Empty Commitment Language
Flag as filler unless followed immediately by specifics:
- "We are committed to..."
- "We are dedicated to..."
- "Our mission is to..."
- "We believe that..."
- "At [Org Name], we..."
- "For [X] years, we have been..."

### Buzzwords & Corporate Fluff
Flag on sight:
- "holistic," "person-centered," "resident-centered" — fine as concepts, but flagged if used as filler
- "seamless," "comprehensive," "robust," "scalable"
- "empower," "transform," "leverage," "synergy," "ecosystem"
- "solutions," "offerings," "deliverables" in a care context
- "journey" used metaphorically ("your care journey")

### Passive / Distance Language
Flag when it creates emotional distance:
- "families are encouraged to..." (instead of "call us" or "talk to our team")
- "residents may..." when the org can be more direct
- Over-use of passive voice to avoid making direct commitments

### Pushy / Pressure Patterns
Flag as pushy:
- "Don't wait," "Act now," "Time is of the essence," "Limited availability"
- "Schedule your tour today!" in a context where the reader hasn't yet understood what they're signing up for
- More than one CTA per section without clear hierarchy
- CTA copy that creates anxiety rather than invitation ("Don't let your loved one go without care")

### Redundancy Triggers
Flag as extraneous:
- Any sentence that starts a section by restating its heading
- The phrase "In conclusion" or "To summarize" in web copy
- Repetition of the same claim (even in different words) within the same page section
- Opening paragraphs that just say what the rest of the page will cover

## Tone Scale by Page Type

Different pages have different appropriate registers. Flag tone mismatches:

| Page Type | Expected Tone | Red Flag if... |
|---|---|---|
| Homepage | Warm, confident, specific | Too formal/clinical, too salesy, leading with awards |
| Facility / Location pages | Grounded, descriptive, inviting | Generic — could be copy-pasted from any facility |
| Services pages | Clear, informative, reassuring | Too clinical, too abstract, missing specifics |
| About / Mission | Authentic, personal, honest | Pure mission-statement language, no human voice |
| Staff / Team pages | Warm, genuine, specific | Generic bio templates, no personality |
| FAQ | Direct, helpful, plain language | Overly formal, non-answers, hedging |
| Contact / Admissions | Calm, helpful, low-pressure | Any urgency language, guilt-trip framing |

## Consistency Checklist

The agent should verify these site-wide:

- [ ] The organization's name is spelled and capitalized consistently on every page
- [ ] Facility names are consistent with the source-of-truth list
- [ ] The same service/program is not described differently on different pages
- [ ] Pronouns are consistent: "we/our" vs "they/their" (org should speak as first person "we" throughout)
- [ ] No page switches between "resident" and "patient" without reason (pick one and use it consistently)
- [ ] No page uses "elderly" — prefer "seniors," "older adults," or "residents"
- [ ] Phone numbers, addresses, and hours appear consistently across all pages where they're listed

## What Good Looks Like

For reference, here is the difference between flagged and acceptable copy:

**❌ Flagged:** "We are committed to providing world-class, holistic, person-centered care to all of our residents in a warm and welcoming environment."

**✅ Acceptable:** "At Beacon, meals are cooked fresh daily and served family-style in the dining room. Residents choose their own activities, schedules, and routines."

---

**❌ Flagged:** "Our industry-leading team of dedicated professionals leverages cutting-edge approaches to deliver exceptional outcomes."

**✅ Acceptable:** "Our care staff has an average tenure of [X] years. Most of our caregivers have worked with the same residents for years — some for more than a decade."

---

**❌ Flagged:** "Don't wait — your loved one deserves the best care available. Schedule a tour today!"

**✅ Acceptable:** "Ready to see if it's the right fit? We'd love to show you around. Tours are available any day of the week."
