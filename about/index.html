NACS Information Technology · NorthPoint

The answer to questions
that never had a place to live.

NorthPoint is a single point of truth for NACS IT — built internally, owned internally, and designed to grow with the organization. It exists because the data always existed. What was missing was a place to aggregate it, reason over it, and act on it.

The Problem

For years, NACS IT operated without a common operating picture. Data existed — in systems, in spreadsheets, in people's heads — but it was siloed, undocumented, or effectively abandoned. Questions that should have had immediate answers required weeks of cross-referencing disconnected sources, if they got answered at all.

Teams worked in parallel, occasionally working against each other — not out of intent, but out of obfuscation by structure. The information asymmetry was built in. No single person, let alone a leadership team, could see the full picture at once.

Long-standing needs went unaddressed. The business case for IT investment was hard to make without data. And without a compelling case, the investment didn't come — which meant the data continued to sit inaccessible, unlevered, and unrealized.

What NorthPoint Is

NorthPoint is a platform for growth, change, and decision-making. A guide and a map. A toolbox. It is not a vendor product, not a consultant's deliverable, and not a project that ends when a contract does.

It is owned by NACS — which means its direction, its data model, and its user experience are determined by operational reality, not by a product team in another city optimizing for another customer's use case.

The Principle

Every data point on this platform was generated by NACS operations. It belongs to NACS. The insight it contains — about staffing ratios, software costs, facility onboarding, risk exposure — is valuable precisely because it is specific to this organization.

When that data lives exclusively inside vendor systems, the vendor controls the interface, the analytics, and the narrative. You pay for access to your own operational intelligence — on their terms, through their lens, and at their price point.

Market Context — Ensign Group
The Ensign Group, one of the largest and most operationally sophisticated SNF operators in the country, built Bridgeway Health Solutions — a proprietary clinical analytics platform — rather than relying solely on third-party EHR vendors for insight. The strategic logic: owning the data pipeline is more defensible than renting access to it. It eliminates vendor dependency on the analytics layer, enables custom UX and workflow decisions that improve staff adoption and retention, and surfaces intelligence that generic platforms aren't designed to surface. NorthPoint operates from the same principle, scaled to NACS.

The near-term roadmap includes connecting NorthPoint to PointClickCare's API — pulling census, utilization, and operational data out of PCC and into a platform NACS controls. Not replacing PCC as the system of record. Building the intelligence layer above it that PCC's generic reporting was never designed to provide.

Where We're Going

NorthPoint is a living platform. What exists today is the foundation. What follows is the aggregation layer — pulling data from the systems NACS already operates, surfacing it through interfaces NACS controls, and building the analytical capability that positions the organization to grow with confidence.
